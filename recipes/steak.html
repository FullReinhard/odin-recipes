Emiya's Hamburg Steak Recipe

Description:

For the show's sixth meal, Shirou Emiya prepares a delicious Hamburg steak for the members of the Emiya household. The dish holds sentimental value for Shirou, as he remembers preparing the meal alongside his guardian, Taiga Fujimura.

Ingredients:

  • 400g (0.88 lbs.) ground meat (70/30 or 60/40 beef/pork)
  • 1.5 medium onions
  • 1 egg
  • 20g (0.7 oz) bread crumbs
  • salt/pepper/nutmeg/oil as needed
  • [A] Simple Sauce:
    • 2 tbsp. Worcestershire sauce
    • 2 tbsp. ketchup
    • 1 tsp. coarse mustard
    • (optional): 2 tbsp. sake
    • (optional): 5g (1 tsp.) butter
    • (optional): pepper to taste
  • [B] Onion Sauce:
    • 1/2 onion
    • 1 small clove garlic
    • 3 tbsp. soy sauce
    • 3 tbsp. vinegar
    • 1 tbsp. sugar
    • 1 tbsp. olive oil

Preparing Onion Sauce

First, mince the onion, then add garlic. Put olive oil in a pan and saute garlic over low heat, making sure not to burn. Lastly, add the onion, soy sauce, vinegar, and sugar to pan. Sauce is ready when it begins to boil.

Steps:

  1. Add a dash of salt to minced onion and saute slowly over low heat until light brown, then chill. Mince half an onion and leave raw.
  2. Add a pinch of salt to still-chilled ground meat in a bowl and mix thoroughly until it begins to stick.
  3. Once meat begins to stick, add bread crumbs, egg, raw onions from [1], pepper, and nutmeg (1-2 dashes), and mix. Split mixtures in fourths and make them into 1.5 cm (0.6 in) thick patties by tossing from palm to palm to remove any air.
  4. Refrigerate formed patties for 30 minutes, letting them sit. (Keeps umami and fat while cooking)
  5. Heat frying pan with oil, then cook patties over medium-low heat (high heat causes patties to shrink.) Once bottom is firm, bring heat to low for 3 minutes. Once edges turn white, flip and cook for another 3 minutes.
  6. Turn heat off and cover with lid or aluminum foil and let sit for 2-3 minutes to allow heat to penetrate the patty. Turn heat back on, then cook for another 2 minutes or more.
  7. Pierce patty with skewer. If juice runs clear, patties are finished cooking. Cook for an additional 15 seconds to sear them, and patties are done.
